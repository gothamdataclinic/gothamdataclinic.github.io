# Activities

We think the best way to realize our vision of a fairer and more responsible future is to
build new tools and curricula to educate, inform, and empower the leaders of tomorrow.

## Developing Educational Materials

Gotham Data Clinic has delivered workshops on data literacy. We've partnered with local
organizations to teach the public how to make sense of data, understand where it comes
from, and make decisions from the information.
Gotham Data Clinic has been developing open-access curricula for computing and data
science education. These courses teach the fundamentals in scientific computing,
exploratory data analysis, statistical inferences, and machine learning techniques. We are
partnering with local colleges and universities to deliver this material. This fits in with
our overall strategy to prepare learners in computing and data science skills.
We have been working as a volunteer-based organization since our incorporation. In
addition, we have not yet charged for workshops so far in our organization’s history. We
were not able to develop and deliver in-person workshops and training during the
pandemic, and we have only now begun to develop in-person direct instruction.
In the near future, we would like to begin to provide paid offerings for workshops and
instruction to generate revenue for our organization to sustain and support new
curriculum development. We are committed to making our course materials freely
available. For those seeking direct instruction, we plan to offer a series of workshops to
develop and master data science skills and a series of workshops to professionally
develop instructors to deliver data science courses.
So far, our workshops have been conducted virtually and the curriculum development has
happened remotely. In the future, we plan to have a co-working space that is dedicated to
the organization where we can host events to the public and have a space to periodically
meet to discuss curriculum development.
It is intended that 34 percent of the organization's time will be dedicated to research and
development of educational content.

## Developing Education Technology

We are actively working to build an education hub to house all of our educational efforts
in one place and provide a computational education for all learners on our platform. We
will host data science educational materials on the hub as well as provide the necessary
computational environment to run data science workloads.
Using some recent advances in the scientific computing ecosystem, there are now tools to
make setting up Python and R, two essential programming languages for data science,
easy to set up and access, all in a web browser. This means anyone with a device that has
a web browser will now be able to learn data science without the need for expensive
equipment. Our hub will greatly increase access to data science education while
dramatically reducing the maintenance costs.
The platform development work is actively being supported by volunteers and we are
creating the hub under a non-commercial license. This will allow us to fulfill our mission
to ensure that the infrastructure remains open while protecting our intellectual property.
We plan to provide a hosted solution for different learning communities and provide
service support for their educational and technical needs. We will charge for maintenance
and support of these hosted solutions and this will provide revenue to support the
platform development and it will allow us to compensate our contributors for their work.
We will also provide a self-hosting option so communities can have full control over their
data and content. We plan to have community spaces on our hub to host and share course
content among instructors. This would create a Github-like environment to share, remix,
and modify lessons and create a forum for instructors to collaborate with one another.
This platform helps fulfill our mission to develop and distribute next-generation
computing and data science education experiences. This platform will make data science
education accessible to everyone.
The development of this work is currently taking place in a remote work environment;
currently we do not have a dedicated space. In the future, we plan to have a dedicated co-
working space to have periodic in-person meetings.
It is intended that 33 percent of the organization's time will be dedicated to technology
development.

## Scholarly and Community Engagement

Gotham Data Clinic is currently working on a landscape analysis and review of data
science education. This scholarly research gives us a perspective of the latest
implementation work happening in secondary education and higher education sectors.
This gives us an evidence-based approach to our curriculum development and allows us
to invest in developing tools that meet the ever-changing needs in computing and data
science. This research helps us fulfill our mission to develop high-quality educational
materials in computing and data science.
We plan to make our findings available and publicize all our work through public
engagements. This includes participating in panel discussions, presentations, and invited
talks. These engagements will give public awareness of our work and allow us to reach
new audiences to fulfill our mission of training the next generation of scientists and
technologists as well as the general public.
This work is currently supported by our volunteers and is done in a remote setting. We
plan to use part of our revenue from workshop delivery and platform hosting to cover the
cost of this integral research. This revenue would also cover the cost of a dedicated co-
working space for convening. It is intended that 33 percent of the organization's time will
be dedicated to scholarly and community engagement.
